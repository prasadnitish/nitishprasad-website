What's actually blocking you.

Each item is tagged with severity and who feels it. Fixes are specific.

01

Amplify has no product evidence

High Hiring manager · flagship

Your own framing positions Amplify as the strongest case study. It's the first project in the hero, has its own proof row, and is the only one with a dedicated "outcomes" panel. And yet the case-study page contains zero pixels of the actual product — no dashboard screenshot, no recommendation card, no system diagram, no mocked-out workflow.

Compare to SproutRoute, which has an assets/screenshots/ folder, a lightbox gallery, and eight SVG diagrams in diagrams/. That page feels like a real product. Amplify reads like a very well-written memo about a product.

"Turned model output into a workflow change that mattered." — but show me the workflow.
FixAdd 2–3 assets to the Amplify page: (a) one UI capture, even redacted/abstracted for NDA; (b) one system diagram showing the hybrid inference + RAG + cache path; (c) one "before vs after" account-manager workflow. If the UI is confidential, draw a Figma-style wireframe of it — reviewers understand that constraint and respect it more than prose. Effort~4 hours. You already built the diagrams for SproutRoute; use the same visual vocabulary. Why it mattersThis is the single biggest delta between "credible" and "impressive" in your portfolio today.
02

Above-the-fold is 90% text, 0% proof

High Recruiter · scan

The first screen is: a nav, a small eyebrow, a giant serif headline, and three paragraphs. A scanning reader has to read ~160 words before they see anything concrete. The "Selected case studies" sidebar helps, but it's another block of text.

Principal-level PM portfolios that land well tend to put one of three things in the hero: a product screenshot, a numbered outcome strip, or a labeled system diagram. You have material for all three. You're using none.

FixCut the intro from 3 paragraphs → 1. Move the metric strip (1,600+ / 99% / <500ms / 45→5 min) directly under the headline. Push one proof artifact into the right column instead of the case-study list. Effort~1 hour in index-app.js.
03

No role targeting in the hero

High Recruiter · match

Recruiters filter by role, level, and location before anything else. Your site tells them this — Principal/Staff PM and AI Product Lead, Seattle or remote, marketplace economics + AI platforms + 0→1 — but buries it at the bottom of the Contact card. By the time it's reached, two-thirds of recruiters have bounced.

FixSurface a short "what I'm looking for" chip row in the hero (next to the eyebrow): Principal / Staff PM · AI platforms · marketplaces · Seattle or remote · Open to new roles. WhyThis is the single piece of text recruiters copy-paste into their ATS notes.
04

"Principal-level" is a self-declared title

Medium Hiring manager · credibility

The hero eyebrow says Principal-level product leader. Your current Amazon title is Senior Product Manager. That gap isn't disqualifying — plenty of PMs are operating above their title — but claiming it on the first line without backing it up is the first thing a skeptical reviewer flags.

You have the evidence to earn the claim (platform scope, VP-facing program ownership, nine-figure budget). Use the evidence, don't lead with the label.

Fix A (conservative)Change eyebrow to Senior PM at Amazon · 0→1 and scale · seeking Principal/Staff. Honest, and the "seeking Principal" is itself a recruiter signal. Fix B (confident)Keep "Principal-level" but add a line of backing: "Principal-level scope — platform ownership, VP program accountability, nine-figure budget."
05

Homepage uses React for a static page

Medium Hiring manager · tech judgment

The homepage mounts React from a CDN and renders entirely static content. Two concerns from a PM-with-technical-depth frame:

(1) SEO & previews. All of the hero text lives in JS. Google renders it eventually, but LinkedIn preview cards, recruiter automation tools, and some ATS crawlers don't. You already invested in per-page og: tags — don't undercut them with a client-rendered homepage.

(2) The signal. A senior PM portfolio that uses React to render static content invites the question "why?" Every case study page is already hand-written HTML. The homepage could be too.

FixPre-render the homepage to static HTML (same output, just build-time). Keep React only for the mobile menu toggle, or replace it with ~10 lines of vanilla JS. Your project-*.html pages already do this. SecondaryOnce static, add a <noscript> path that still shows the hero.
06

Case-study pages have no consistent template

Medium Hiring manager · comparability

Amplify has: Quick Read, What I owned, Scope, Deep Dive. SproutRoute has: Why It Was Hard, System Evolution, Latency, Decisions. Sprout Math has: Quick Read, Visuals, Deep Dive. Every case study invents its own structure.

For a reader comparing three projects back-to-back, the cognitive tax is real — they have to re-learn the IA each click. A shared 5-section template (Problem · Tradeoff · What I did · Outcome · What I'd do differently) would let reviewers skim laterally across projects and compare like-for-like.

FixPick one canonical spine (the 5 above is a safe default). Keep project-specific sections as optional extras after the spine, not in place of it.
07

"What I'd do differently" is missing everywhere

Medium Hiring manager · senior signal

At Principal/Staff level, the thing that separates "ran a project" from "has product judgment" is self-critique. None of your case studies have a retrospective section. Adding one — honest, not humble-brag — is the highest-leverage single addition for the deep-read audience.

"If I ran Amplify again, I'd spend the first two weeks on the account-manager ride-alongs instead of the data model. The data was never the bottleneck; the workflow framing was."

That kind of line does more for your candidacy than another outcome metric.

08

Outcome metrics need source / denominator context

Medium Hiring manager · credibility

"99% infrastructure cost reduction ($60K → $500)" is a great headline — and also the kind of number an experienced reviewer sanity-checks. Against what baseline? Annualized? Per-user? The Amplify deep dive hints at the architecture change, but the metric needs one line of attribution next to it.

Same with "45 → 5 min per AM call." Measured how — self-report, instrumented, sampled?

FixAdd a small footnote or tooltip under each metric: baseline, measurement method, time window. Three words each is fine. This also protects you in interviews; the question is coming anyway.
09

"Senior Product Manager" doesn't include Principal-level context

Low Recruiter · level calibration

Amazon L7 = Principal PM; Sr PM is typically L6. A recruiter who doesn't know Amazon levels can't tell which bracket you're in from the résumé alone. If you're targeting external Principal/Staff roles, the résumé summary should name the scope in level-neutral terms (ICs influenced, surface owned, budget, VP-line visibility) rather than relying on the title.

10

Mobile: hero font-size is too large, metrics wrap poorly

Low Both · mobile

On a 375px viewport the serif headline takes up a full screen by itself and pushes everything below the fold. The 45→5 min proof tile breaks awkwardly because of the arrow glyph. ~40% of portfolio traffic is mobile (phone-sent LinkedIn links); this matters more than it feels like.

FixClamp the headline tighter: clamp(1.9rem, 8vw, 3.6rem). Use non-breaking arrow (&nbsp;→&nbsp;) or swap "→" for "to" on small screens.
11

Icons add noise, not meaning

Low UX · visual

Each project card has a unicode glyph: ◉, △, □, ✦, ◇, ▣. They don't map to the products (△ for SproutRoute is arguably a route, but ◉ for Amplify = ?) and they're inconsistent in weight. Either drop them entirely, replace with real icons that map to the product category, or commit and make them a visual system (same weight, same spacing, same color).

12

"Supporting Systems" is a weak section title

Low Copy · framing

The Eval Control Tower, RAG pipeline, and AI Safety Audit are the projects that most directly demonstrate AI PM technical depth — which is exactly what the roles you're targeting screen for. "Supporting Systems" makes them sound like B-side work.

FixRename to "AI systems I built to answer my own product questions" or "Tools I built, not papers I read". Move this section higher — directly under Amplify — if targeting AI platform roles.
13

What works — keep these

Strengths Preserve
  • Voice. "I like the stage where the request is still fuzzy." Reads as a senior operator, not an MBA deck.
  • Specific numbers. $60K→$500, 1,600+ AMs, 45→5 min. Non-generic, memorable, defensible (once you add the baseline note in #08).
  • Cross-linking. Every case study has a live demo or GitHub link in the nav. That single pattern is worth more than most of the prose.
  • SproutRoute diagrams. The eight SVG architecture diagrams are the best single asset on the site. Treat them as the quality bar for the other projects.
  • Honest scope. "Senior PM" + "Principal-level" + "Open to Principal/Staff" is an internally consistent story once #04 is fixed.

One more thing

The site is a sampling problem, not a rewriting problem.

The prose is already above the bar. The material underneath it — Amplify's real architecture, the actual Salesforce path tradeoff, a redacted workflow screenshot, a retrospective paragraph — is what most PM candidates at this level never produce. Publishing even 30% of it, with the same writing discipline you've already applied, lifts the site from "competent" to "the strongest PM portfolio in the stack."

The ratio to aim for: less telling, more showing. Every sentence that describes a system should be next to a picture of that system. Every claim should be next to a baseline. Every outcome should be next to the workflow it changed.
